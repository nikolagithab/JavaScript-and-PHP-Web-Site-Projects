My story began 19 years ago in Scarborough, Ontario when I immigrated from Macedonia. I had a passion for technology and few part-time jobs to get me started. My vision was simple – to apply my skills and knowledge in a world-class economy.

I was an international student. I served tables in banquet facilities, took drive-thru orders at Tim Hortons, completed cash register transactions for clothing retailers – and managed to graduate with distinction from a private college for pharmaceutical manufacturing technology. Despite struggling in the first 5 years, I gained valuable customer service and technical skills.

Some of my first hi-tech placements included liquids packaging technician and solid-dose manufacturing technician roles at GlaxoSmithKline in Mississauga, Ontario.

My career further developed through providing contract pharmaceutical manufacturing services as packaging engineering specialist at Patheon in Mississauga, Ontario. The product development functions I supported there are different than software development processes – but the methodology used in problem analysis, product design, technical documentation and quality standards are the same. To me, there is no greater value than fostering a quality culture. It’s my belief that details create quality product.

When my previous company restructured, my position was terminated. But I moved on with my career to Sheridan’s Faculty of Applied Science and Technology in Oakville, Ontario. I chose a new computer programmer occupation because I love automation of business processes by combining the power of IT with an in-dept understanding of business. I learned. I worked hard. And I re-qualified.

Over my 19-year journey, the industry has become more information-driven and sophisticated, but so have I. By graduating from two colleges and immersing myself in the industry I have expanded my technical skills and specialized in new areas of information communication technology.

Today I am proud to say that I have been selected for the Ryerson University Advanced Digital and Professional Training (ADaPT) internship program.
